Executive Summary

Tom Malinowski, a former U.S. Congressman and Assistant Secretary of State, entered the NJ-11 special election primary in November 2025. With 18 days to the primary, the campaign faced a critical challenge: when New Jersey voters asked Google, ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity about Malinowski, they were seeing ethics complaints and stock trading violations first—burying his blind trust resolution and extensive foreign policy credentials.

Kyanos deployed three integrated services to provide actionable intelligence: Panopticon monitored consumer AI platforms (Google AI Overview, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Bing Copilot) to capture exactly what voters see when they research candidates. Adwatch tracked political advertising across all candidates and Super PACs. GoodInk discovered positive press coverage ready to feature on the campaign website.

The analysis revealed that Google AI Overview (Grade D) doesn't mention the current campaign, Perplexity leads with "violated the STOCK Act" on trust questions, and the campaign website's news section was last updated in 2020—leaving AI systems without fresh positive content to surface.

Critical Consumer AI Response

What Perplexity Tells Voters About Honesty

When a voter asks Perplexity "Is Tom Malinowski honest?", the platform leads with ethics violations—before mentioning diplomatic career or blind trust resolution.

Actual Perplexity Response (perplexity.ai)
"Tom Malinowski violated the STOCK Act by failing to disclose over 100 stock trades worth $1.3M-$5M from 2019-2021, including pandemic-timed medical/tech sales, prompting Office of Congressional Ethics findings of 'knowing' non-compliance."
Why AI Says This — Sources Cited
  • campaignlegal.org — Campaign Legal Center ethics complaint (hostile framing)
  • businessinsider.com — "Pandemic profiteering" narrative
  • Wikipedia — Controversy section prominently placed

What's missing: The campaign website has no FAQ or ethics page addressing this directly. AI has no authoritative source presenting the campaign's framing.

What You Control: Campaign Website
Publish FAQ page at malinowskifornj.com/ethics addressing: (1) Blind trust established July 2021, (2) No fines or penalties issued, (3) Support for congressional stock trading ban. This gives AI an authoritative source to cite alongside the negative coverage.
Critical Consumer AI Response

What Gemini Tells Voters About Trust

Google's Gemini devotes the first several paragraphs to the "2021 Stock Trading Controversy" before discussing remediation steps.

Actual Gemini Response (gemini.google.com)
"In the context of his 2026 campaign for New Jersey's 11th District, questions about Tom Malinowski's honesty usually center on a specific financial disclosure controversy... Reports revealed Malinowski failed to disclose over 100 stock trades worth between $1.3 million and $5 million within the 45-day window required by the STOCK Act."
Positive: Gemini Also Shows Remediation

Gemini does mention the blind trust and support for stock trading bans—but this appears after the controversy framing. The campaign website could provide a source that leads with the resolution, not the violation.

What You Control: Campaign Website
Add "Ethics and Accountability" section with clear timeline: violation → investigation → blind trust (2021) → reform advocacy. Lead with "Malinowski placed assets in blind trust and sponsored congressional stock trading ban." This reframes the narrative for AI.
High Consumer AI Gap

Google AI Overview Missing Current Campaign

Google AI Overview—the AI summary at the top of Google Search—only provides historical information. No mention of NJ-11, February 2026, or current candidacy.

Actual Google AI Overview (google.com)
"Tomasz Pobóg 'Tom' Malinowski is an American politician and former diplomat who served as the U.S. representative from New Jersey's 7th congressional district from 2019 to 2023."
Why AI Says This — Sources Cited
  • Wikipedia — Article focuses on 2019-2023 term, current campaign buried
  • congress.gov — Historical record only

What's missing: Campaign website lacks schema.org markup and clear "currently running for NJ-11" language in meta description. Google doesn't see strong signals about current campaign.

What You Control: Campaign Website
Update homepage meta description to lead with "Democratic candidate for NJ-11 special election (Feb 2026)". Add schema.org/Person markup with "jobTitle": "Candidate for U.S. Congress". This helps Google AI Overview surface current candidacy.
High Consumer AI Gap

Immigration Position Missing Across All Platforms

Voters asking about Malinowski's immigration stance get nothing—despite his compelling personal story (fled Communist Poland at age 6). Immigration is a top 2026 issue.

Actual ChatGPT Response (chatgpt.com)
"Tom Malinowski... focusing on issues like housing, sustainability, and transportation." [No immigration position]
Why AI Says This — Source Gap

The campaign website (malinowskifornj.com) has no /issues/immigration page. AI systems have no authoritative source for his immigration position. His immigrant story appears in some profiles but isn't connected to policy.

What You Control: Campaign Website
Create malinowskifornj.com/issues/immigration with: (1) Personal story—fled Communist Poland age 6, (2) DACA position, (3) Human Rights Watch refugee work. This is a uniquely compelling narrative that's currently invisible to AI.
High Consumer AI Gap

Andy Kim Endorsement Not Consistently Surfacing

Meta AI surfaces the Andy Kim endorsement, but Perplexity and Gemini often don't. Some platforms lead with Gill's Essex County backing instead.

Actual Meta AI Response (meta.ai) — Working
"He's endorsed by Senator Andy Kim and has a voting record supporting environmental protection, labor rights, and social equality."
Why Inconsistent — Source Analysis

Meta AI finds the endorsement in New Jersey Globe coverage. But the campaign website has no /endorsements page and the homepage doesn't mention Andy Kim. Platforms that rely more heavily on official campaign sources miss it.

What You Control: Campaign Website
Create malinowskifornj.com/endorsements with structured data markup. Add "Endorsed by U.S. Senator Andy Kim" to homepage hero. This ensures ALL AI platforms can find this information from the authoritative source.
Working Amplify Positive

Human Rights Credentials Consistently Highlighted

Positive finding: All major consumer AI platforms consistently mention Human Rights Watch and State Department service. This narrative is working—build on it.

Actual Gemini Response (gemini.google.com)
"Before holding elected office, Malinowski was the Washington Director for Human Rights Watch (2001–2013). In this role, he was a leading advocate for ending the use of torture by the U.S. government and promoted democratic reforms globally."
Why This Works — Source Analysis

Wikipedia, the campaign website, and multiple news sources all prominently feature this credential. The consistent sourcing means AI platforms reliably surface it. This is the model for other topics.

What You Control: Expand This Pattern
Connect human rights credentials to current issues: immigration (personal story), democracy defense (anti-authoritarianism), and ethics (transparency advocate). Apply the same source consistency to topics that aren't working.
