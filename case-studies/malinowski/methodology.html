Platform Coverage

Primary Platforms: Google AI Overview + ChatGPT

This report focuses on the two AI surfaces that matter most for voter information:

Primary Google AI Overview

The AI-generated summary box at the top of Google Search results. Millions of voters see this first when searching for candidate information. Powered by Google's search index.

Primary ChatGPT

The most widely used AI assistant with 100M+ users. Increasingly used for political research. Uses real-time web search to answer questions about candidates.

Why These Two Matter Most

Google AI Overview appears in the search results that voters already use. ChatGPT is where curious voters go for deeper research. Together, they represent the dominant surfaces where voters encounter AI-generated political information.

A Note on Meta AI and WhatsApp

WhatsApp is increasingly used for political information sharing, particularly in Latino and immigrant communities. We cannot directly monitor WhatsApp because it presents AI only on mobile devices. We are adding Meta AI monitoring as a proxy—the underlying model is the same, so improvements that affect Meta AI should affect WhatsApp AI as well.

Why Improving Google + ChatGPT Improves Everything

Our experience shows that focusing on Google AI Overview and ChatGPT creates improvements that propagate to other platforms. Here's why:

  • Shared Source Material All AI platforms pull from overlapping sources: the campaign website, Wikipedia, news coverage. Improving these sources improves all platforms.
  • Google Indexing is Foundational Platforms like Perplexity and ChatGPT use Google's index or similar web crawls. Better Google presence means better presence everywhere.
  • Recency Signals Transfer Fresh content that Google prioritizes also gets prioritized by other platforms looking for current information.
  • Source Authority is Universal An authoritative source (official campaign website, major news outlet) carries weight across all AI systems.
The Practical Implication

You don't need to optimize for every platform separately. Fix the fundamentals—website content, SEO, fresh press coverage—and improvements propagate across surfaces. We recommend starting with Google and ChatGPT, measuring results, then expanding monitoring only if specific platform issues persist.

How We Monitor

Consumer Reality, Not API Fiction

We monitor what voters actually see, not what AI APIs return to developers. This distinction matters:

  • Real Browser Sessions We use actual consumer browser sessions, not API calls. AI platforms often return different results to consumers than to developers.
  • Geographic Targeting Queries are executed from locations within the district. Search results and AI responses can vary by location.
  • Source Attribution We track which sources AI platforms cite. Understanding where information comes from reveals how to change it.

How We Source Test Queries

We test the questions voters actually ask—not questions we imagine they might ask. Here's how we build each study's query set:

  • Google Autocomplete We start with what Google suggests when voters type the candidate's name. These suggestions reflect actual search behavior—what real people are looking for right now.
  • Reddit & Discord Coming Soon We're adding analysis of political discussions on Reddit and Discord to identify emerging topics and concerns before they show up in search data.
  • Campaign-Requested Prompts Coming Soon Campaigns will be able to request monitoring of specific questions—topics they're hearing on the ground, attack lines they want to track, or policy areas they want to own.
Why This Matters

Testing real voter queries means we catch problems that matter. If voters aren't asking about a topic, an AI error on that topic is low priority. If autocomplete shows voters are searching "[candidate] scandal," that's a problem worth fixing—even if the campaign wishes they weren't.

Integrated Intelligence

AI monitoring is most valuable when combined with advertising and press intelligence:

  • Advertising Context What are opponents saying in paid media? Attack ad narratives can influence AI responses when they generate press coverage or social sharing.
  • Press Coverage Tracking What positive coverage exists that AI should be citing? What gaps exist that the campaign should fill?
  • Website Analysis What pages exist on the campaign website? What's missing? What SEO signals is the site sending to search engines and AI?

This integrated approach lets us explain not just what AI says, but why it says it—and what specific actions will change it.

The Math

Traditional advertising costs $50-200 per voter moved. Fixing AI presence costs a fraction of that and reaches voters at the moment they're actively researching—when they're most persuadable. A campaign spending $300,000 on TV ads to fix a reputation problem could achieve similar results by spending $10,000 on website improvements and AI monitoring.

Important caveat: These estimates are based on available research and reasonable assumptions. Actual impact will vary based on race competitiveness, voter engagement, and how effectively fixes are implemented. We recommend tracking AI responses before and after changes to measure real impact.
