    📊 Financial Plan

    Objectives

    Our financial plan is designed to achieve three strategic objectives that ensure both mission success and sustainable operations:

    🎯 Maximum Impact

    Ensure Democratic facts and values are discoverable in digital and AI environments, creating measurable improvements in candidate representation across AI platforms.

    💰 Sustainable Economics

    Operate as a stable organization that can carry through off-cycle years without perpetual fundraising, maintaining core capabilities between election cycles.

    🏆 Talent Attraction

    Offer competitive compensation and a mission-driven culture strong enough to recruit and retain top-tier political technology talent in Washington DC market.

    All financial assumptions below serve these objectives and are subject to validation through market research and operational experience.

    Financing Roadmap

    Staged Investment Strategy

    We stage our financing to match political cycles and give investors clear checkpoints for performance validation and risk management.1

    Seed (2025)
    $500K

    Purpose: Complete core product development and run pilot projects with 5-10 early adopter campaigns.[^2]

    Series A (2026)
    $2.5M

    Purpose: Expand engineering team, establish VAR (value-added reseller) partnerships, and scale customer success operations.[^3]

    Convertible Debt (2028)
    $2.2M

    Purpose: Liquidity during the presidential cycle – the costliest year – without premature dilution.[^4]

    This approach provides optionality: if the model does not prove out, investors can cut losses after 2026 or 2027. If it succeeds, the additional capital is ready to scale.

    Gross vs. Net Revenue Model

    We distinguish between gross revenue (fees campaigns pay) and net revenue (what we keep after VAR commissions):

    Revenue Structure

    • Gross revenue is the full subscription and services fee billed to campaigns, committees, and officeholders
    • VAR commissions (≈25% by 2028) are paid to distribution partners who bring in customers, onboard them, and provide first-line support5
    • Net revenue is what we keep to fund product development, compliance, and higher-level support

    Strategic Advantage: VARs extend reach into hundreds of campaigns we could never serve directly, while our retained net revenue funds the moat – schema development, platform integrations, and bias monitoring capabilities.

    Financial Plan Validation & Monitoring

    Validation Requirements

    Like any financial forecast, this plan represents assumptions layered upon assumptions that require systematic validation through research and operational experience. Our validation roadmap includes:

    Customer Discovery & Market Validation:

    • Structured interviews with 50+ Democratic campaign managers across all tiers to validate pricing acceptance, value proposition, and penetration rate assumptions
    • Pilot program with 5-10 early adopter campaigns to test actual usage patterns, customer success requirements, and retention metrics
    • Competitive benchmarking against existing political technology companies to validate revenue models, growth patterns, and market positioning

    Partnership & Distribution Research:

    • VAR partnership investigation with established political consulting firms to validate commission structures, relationship management capabilities, and market reach assumptions
    • Platform integration validation with WordPress, NationBuilder, and NGP VAN to confirm technical feasibility and implementation costs
    • Channel partner interviews to understand political technology distribution patterns and relationship management requirements

    Financial Model Stress Testing:

    • Sensitivity analysis on key assumptions including penetration rates (±10%), pricing acceptance (±20%), and operational costs (±15%)
    • Scenario planning for delayed market adoption, increased competition, or regulatory changes affecting business model viability
    • Cash flow modeling under various funding timing scenarios to ensure operational continuity during validation periods

    🔍 Continuous Monitoring & Plan Adjustment

    We will carefully monitor our progress against these financial projections and adjust our plan accordingly as we gather market intelligence:

    • Monthly financial reviews comparing actual performance to projections with variance analysis and corrective action planning
    • Quarterly assumption validation reassessing market penetration rates, pricing acceptance, and competitive positioning based on customer feedback and market research
    • Annual model updates incorporating lessons learned, market changes, and strategic pivots into revised financial projections and funding requirements
    • Investor reporting with transparent variance analysis, assumption validation progress, and plan adjustments to maintain stakeholder confidence and support

    Adaptation Triggers: We will revise financial assumptions when actual performance varies >20% from projections for two consecutive quarters, or when market research indicates fundamental assumption changes are required.
