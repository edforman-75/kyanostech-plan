    Go-to-Market Strategy

    Phase 1: Foundation (Q1-Q2 2026)

    Objectives

    • Secure $1M target funding by March 15
    • Finalize trust structure with progressive board
    • Hire core team (5 FTEs: CEO, Technical Lead, Research Director, Operations Manager, Marketing & Partnerships)

    Activities

    • Foundation outreach (Open Society, Tides, Way to Win, others)
    • Labor union conversations (SEIU, AFSCME, AFL-CIO political programs)
    • Family office and major donor cultivation

    Phase 2: Free Year (Q2-Q4 2026)

    Objectives

    • Serve 200 progressive campaigns at no cost
    • Generate case studies and testimonials
    • Build credibility for 2027 paid launch

    Activities

    • Q2: Founding cohort (first 50 campaigns with hands-on support)
    • Q3: Open enrollment (additional campaigns as capacity allows)
    • Q4: Case studies published; 2027 pricing announced

    2026 Target Mix

    Level Clients Focus
    Statewide 15 High-visibility case studies
    Congress 50 Competitive House races
    Local 110 State leg, major city
    Officeholders 15 2024 winners transitioning
    Organizations 10 Consultant partners
    Total 200

    Phase 3: Revenue Launch (2027)

    Objectives

    • Activate paid pricing
    • Reach cash-flow positive by Q2
    • Generate $1.2M revenue

    Activities

    • Convert 2026 free clients to paid
    • Expand consultant partnerships
    • Launch commercial services revenue

    Phase 4: Full Scale (2028)

    Objectives

    • 466+ customers
    • $3.3M revenue
    • Presidential campaign engagement

    Activities

    • Full product suite deployment
    • National-level service delivery
    • Research Institute launch

    Channel Strategy

    Two Distinct Motions

    Segment Channel Support Model
    Small Local Direct to campaign Self-service
    Congress+ Through consultants Consultant-supported

    By Customer Count

    Channel % of Customers Notes
    Direct self-service (small Local) 40% High volume, low touch
    Consultant partners (Congress+) 35% Consultant manages relationship
    PAC/committee sponsored 20% Volume deals for multiple campaigns
    Referrals 5% Word of mouth

    By Revenue

    Channel % of Revenue
    Consultant partners 50%
    PAC/committee sponsored 30%
    Direct self-service 15%
    Referrals 5%

    Consultants are the primary revenue channel because they bring larger races with higher price points.

    Consultant Partnership Model

    Why Consultants

    • They already have trusted relationships with campaigns
    • They make technology recommendations
    • They provide support layer we don't have to build
    • They become a sales channel

    How It Works

    1. We train consultants on Kyanos products
    2. Consultant recommends Kyanos to their campaign clients
    3. Consultant provides implementation support
    4. We provide technical support to consultant
    5. Consultant earns commission on referred revenue

    Value to Consultants

    • Differentiated service offering
    • Commission income
    • Early access to AI monitoring capabilities
    • Training and certification

    Self-Service Model (Local Races)

    Design Principles

    • Zero friction onboarding: Campaign enters info, system configures automatically
    • Dashboard-first: All insights available without human support
    • CMS-specific instructions: Copy-paste fixes for WordPress, Squarespace, etc.
    • Knowledge base: Self-serve help for common questions

    Support Tiers

    Tier Response Cost
    Knowledge base Instant Included
    Email support 48 hours Included
    Priority email 24 hours Upgrade
